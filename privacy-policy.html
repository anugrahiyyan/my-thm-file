Privacy Policy

Last Updated: 01 November 2024

At ONEderland Enterprise, we are committed to protecting the privacy and security of all data stored within our systems. This Privacy Policy outlines the practices and measures we follow to ensure that data related to our asset management operations, including personal and equipment-related information, is kept safe, secure, and confidential.

1. Data Security

We take your data security seriously. Our servers are hosted on an Ubuntu-based system, which has been configured to meet industry standards for security. Key security measures include:

  • Encryption: All sensitive data is encrypted using advanced encryption methods to ensure that it is secure both in transit and at rest. This ensures that any unauthorized access attempts are rendered ineffective.
  • Secure Access: Access to our system is protected by multi-factor authentication (MFA), strong password policies, and role-based permissions to ensure only authorized personnel can access sensitive information.
  • Server Security: Our server is regularly updated with the latest security patches and monitored to detect any potential vulnerabilities. Firewalls, intrusion detection systems (IDS), and secure SSH access are in place to further enhance security.

2. Data Collection and Usage

We collect and store data related to our company assets, including:

  • Equipment details (such as laptops, PCs, and peripherals)
  • Employee assignments and asset usage history
  • Maintenance schedules and records

This data is collected solely for asset management purposes and is not shared with third parties unless explicitly required by law.

3. Data Retention

All data will be retained for as long as it is necessary to fulfill the purpose for which it was collected or as required by legal obligations. Regular audits are conducted to ensure that unnecessary or outdated data is securely deleted.

4. Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Access any data related to assets assigned to you.
  • Request corrections or updates to your personal information.
  • Request the deletion of personal data when it is no longer required, subject to our legal obligations.

5. Data Protection Practices

Our team follows strict internal guidelines to handle all data with care. Regular security training is provided to employees, and access to sensitive data is limited to authorized personnel only.

6. Third-Party Services

We do not share your data with third parties except for services that are integral to the operation of our business, such as backup services. All third-party services we engage with are vetted to ensure they adhere to similar privacy and security standards.

7. Continuous Security Improvements

We are committed to continuously improving the security of our systems. Our Ubuntu server environment is regularly monitored, and we take prompt action to address any emerging security concerns. Regular audits and penetration testing are conducted to ensure ongoing protection.
